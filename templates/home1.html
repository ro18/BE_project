Interview Coaching

“Death will be a great relief. No more interviews.”

Job Interviews are a stressful part of almost every student’s growth which plays a crucial part in securing a good future for oneself. Without any particular exceptions students or candidates usually scrutinize the subject/topic content relevant to their interview. While we usually presume content is an important parameter in judging a candidate’s potential, the reality is far from it. Research conducted by Nalini Ambady and Robert Rosenthal (1993), experimental psychologists at Harvard University, showed that rock-solid impressions are made within the first 20 seconds of an interview. Albert Mehrabian, a pioneer researcher of body language in the 1950's, found that the total impact of a message is about 55% nonverbal, 38% vocal and only 7% verbal.
1

Facial Component

“When your face says it all, your mouth waits its turn.”

Emotion recognition is one of the main features for identifying the candidate’s traits suitable for the job as well as deciding the results of the interview. For this purpose, the dataset used is the Facial Expression Recognition 2013 (FER-2013) dataset. The data consists of 35,685 examples that are 48*48 pixel grayscale images of faces. The images are categorized into seven categories namely angry, disgust, fear, happy, sad, surprise and neutral ranked from 0 to 6 respectively. OpenCV is used to display a square box on the boundaries of the facedetected using Haar Cascade Classifiers. According to the face detected in the video, one of the categories with a greater probability is calculated and displayed on the screen.
2

Prosodic Component

"It takes the human voice to render depth to words."

This component reflects the person’s tone modulation and thus portrays his social intent regardless of the verbal cues. We extract these features using a model built on the CREDA dataset based on an SVM model using open source library pyaudio analysis as its basis. Prosodic features are collected over each single question duration and then compiled together to form a complete emotion profile of the interview.
3

Textual Component

“Words can inspire. And words can destroy. Choose yours well.”

Our efforts are concentrated on recognizing social intent hidden within the candidate interview. We extract lexical features by using IBM Personality Insights REST API [15] which provides best in class accuracy. The lexical features are analyzed over each individual answer duration and then a composite picture averaging this analysis is provided for the interview.This component provides insights on the lines of five big personality characteristics i.e. agreeableness, conscientiousness, extraversion, emotional range and openness. These characteristics are coupled with two other dimensions, needs and values. The model provides us with 12 characteristic needs and 5 characteristic values.

Inspiration and Need

"Failures can be needs and inspirations."

We all had our College Placement Interviews where we faced a lot of problems, not just because of our incompetence, but due to the human element of judement from a prejudiced eye that may be affected by physical appearance, gender bias or personal mood. This drove us to create a software to replace human beings as interviewers but that is way beyond our grasps, hence this software was developed as a stepping stone to that goal.
